Tools

Mandatory

Source Content Management

Why
A good scm allow you to:
  • Save your work and rollback easily
  • Keep trace of what you did, and how.
  • Implement features easily: branch, so you don't break your project during development, and you can still commit.
  • Share, and work together.
What
  • git or GTFO

Tickets

Why
  • For bug reports.
  • For feature request.
  • As a todo list/backlog.
What
  • Redmine/chiliproject
  • Jira
  • Avoid trac if your project contains subprojects

Unit test framework

Why
Unit test are awesome for:
  • Debugging.
  • Avoid regressions.
  • They give nice examples of how to use your code.

Packager

Why
If you want your project to work you need to make it easy to test. One way is to package it nicely.
What
If your whatever language, framework, does not provide it:
  • cpack (which can be used without cmake)
  • fpm
  • Be a man and right your own rules/specs/ebuild

Nice to have

Continuous integration automate

Why
  • It nice that it works on your computer, but it is better if it works on main targets (provides a packages for each target).
  • Runs all the tools bellow in background.
What
  • Jenkins
  • gitlab-ci
  • buildbot

Static analyser

Why
Efficient quality checking and nice advice at a low price. What's else?
What
  • clang/gcc
  • cppcheck/clint
  • sonar
